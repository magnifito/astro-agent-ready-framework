# AI tells — reference list

Read this during a rewrite pass. Source: field observation + Edward Sturm's "How to Humanize ChatGPT Content" (edwardsturm.com), extended with structural patterns.

## Buzzwords (replace with the plain word, or delete)

delve, delved, embark(ed), leverage, elevate, enhance, foster, empower, unleash, unlock,
crucial, pivotal, invaluable, groundbreaking, relentless, enlightening, esteemed,
insights, expertise, deep understanding, shed light, resonate, amplify, adhere,
intricate, tapestry, synergy, landscape, navigate (metaphorical), realm, robust,
seamless(ly), streamline(d), game-changer, cutting-edge, state-of-the-art,
holistic, comprehensive, myriad, plethora, "a wide range of", "in the realm of",
endeavour/endeavor (as verb), utilize (→ use), facilitate (→ help/let),
"actually" as filler (~90% of instances cuttable), "essentially", "basically",
"arguably", "notably", "importantly", "significantly" without a number.

## Phrase-level tells

- "In today's fast-paced / ever-evolving world..."
- "It's important to note that…" / "It's worth noting…"
- "Let's dive in" / "Let's explore"
- "In conclusion" / "Ultimately" / "At the end of the day" / "In summary"
- "It's not just X — it's Y" (contrast scaffold)
- "Whether you're a X or a Y…" (fake-audience opener)
- "…and more!" / "the possibilities are endless"
- "This isn't about X. It's about Y."
- "take your X to the next level"
- "unlock the full potential of"
- "a testament to"
- "plays a vital/crucial role in"
- "when it comes to"
- Sycophantic transitions: "Great question", "Absolutely!"

## Structural tells

- Uniform paragraph lengths; metronomic sentence rhythm (~20 words each).
- Rule-of-three everywhere: "clear, direct, and natural."
- Em dash density >1 per paragraph. (The glyph is fine; the frequency is the tell.)
- Bold-term-colon bullets for everything: "**Speed:** it is fast."
- Every section same shape: intro sentence → 3 bullets → wrap-up sentence.
- Headers in Title Case for minor subsections; header for every 2 paragraphs.
- Balanced hedging on every claim ("While X, it is also important to consider Y").
- Summary/conclusion paragraph restating the piece.
- Zero first person, zero opinion, zero specific incident.
- Emoji bullets or emoji-decorated headers.
- Numbered lists where order doesn't matter.
- "H2 as a question?" repeated for every section (fine for FAQ/AEO pages — a tell in essays).

## What humans do that AI doesn't (add these back)

- Commit to a claim without hedging; flag uncertainty on ONE specific point instead.
- Specific numbers, names, dates, costs, versions — even imperfect ones.
- A single first-person anecdote with a consequence.
- Asides in parentheses (like this one) with personality.
- Sentence fragments. For emphasis.
- Mild irritation, humor, or admitted confusion.
- References to the reader's actual situation, not "businesses of all sizes".

## Notes on detectors

gptzero.me, zerogpt.com, grammarly.com/ai-detector exist; users may mention them. Don't optimize against them — false positives on human text are common and passing them proves little. Optimize for the read-aloud test and the tells above. If a user demands "passes detector X", explain the unreliability, do the humanization properly, and let them test.
